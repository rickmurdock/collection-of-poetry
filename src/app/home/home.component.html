Poem Categories

Poems that were published in the Orlando Sentinel and the Orlando Evening Star in the 1930's and early 1940's.

The High Cost of Living

We blamed it on the Army and on the Army worm,
We blamed it on the hoof and mouth or on some other germ,
We blamed it on the butcher, we blamed it on the baker,
We blamed it on the President and on the undertaker,
We blamed it on the tariff, the mother-in-law and cook,
We blamed it on the sheriff and on the scheming crook,
We blamed it, too, on Wall Street and on the corporations,
We blamed it on our Navy's fleet and on the other nations,
We blamed it, too, on Christmas giving, but sometime before we die,
We will find the cost of living was due to living high.

A.H.S

One True Friend

A. H. Schilke

    From the depths of my soul this cry goes out,
      A cry that some hearts might rend,
    A cry this is mingled with anguish and doubt,
      `Tis the cry for one true friend.

    There was never a soul so steeped in sin,
      But came out all right in the end.
    If they had but a spark of love within,
      And only one true friend.

It's Not What
You Have


  It's now what you have that makes you great,
   That makes you rich or poor.
  It's not what you have that will open the gate,
    To the city of the pure.

  It's not what you have that will make you strong,
    To fight life's battles here.
  It's not what you have will right the wrong,
    Or dry the flowering tear.

  It's not what you have, but what you are,
    Will make your calling sure.
  Will pass you at the judgement bar,
    To the City of the Pure.
                                          -A.H.S

The Still Small Voice of God

A. H. Schilke

    Hath the spirit that speaketh in tongues of His choice
    Not spoke to you in the still small voice?
      Did He wisper not of a needy soul
      Struggling, bleeding, to reach the goal?
    As you prayed your prayer so long and loud
    Did you hear the voice of teh thundercloud?
      Or the voice of the Spirit speak low to thee
      Saying "Help this soul, he is dear to Me"?
    Were you zealous for God and hid in a cave
    While the Spirit was pleading this soul to save?
      Were you seaking God on the mountain spire
      In the trembling quake of the roaring fire?
    As you stood transfigured upon the mount
    Did you build three temples of no account?
      It is well to bask in the heaven's white glow
      But don't miss the cry in the valley below,
    For the realationship of human need
    Is greater than ties to blood or creed.
      For God is not seeking for leaders to-day
      But for followers to follow the Calvary way.
    Not for judges to sit and stern justice to mete
    But for witnesses true at His mercy seat;
      Not for vessels of silver or vessels of gold
      But vessels of clay this treasure to hold
    It was not the voice of an angel or bird
    But the still small Voice that Elijah heard.
      And the Voice that speaks to you and me 
      Is speaking to sould across the sea
    Not the voice of Abel that spoke from the sod
    But the still small Voice, the voice of God.

He Is No Friend of Mine

A. H. Schilke

 
        He who lets me blindly go,
        Lets me thorns and thistles sow,
        Lets me drink my cup of woe -
            He is no friend of mine.

        He who sees my secret fault,
        And lets me go nor calls of halt,
        But lets me go in wild revolt -
            He is no friend of mine.

        He who pats me on the back,
        While I tead the downward track,
        And then he says, Alas, alack!
            He is no friend of mine.
        
        He who whispers pretty things,
        And says tha all I need is wings,
        Yet never speaks the truth which sings -
            He is no friend of mine.

I Shall Not Live in
Vain

A. H. Schilke

          If I can make some burden lighter,
          Or some lone pathway brighter,
          Or some hungry soul can feed,
          Not by proxy nor by creed,
          But can meet the it's utmost need
          By a thought, a word, or deed,
            I shall not live in vain.

          For the Maser said of old
          That a cup of water cold
          Given in My Name alone,
          Though for sin cannot atone;
          Yet the deed I'll surely own
          When I sit upon my thrown
            Through eternity to reign.
